# Skill-Forge Badge Design Specifications

## Design Philosophy

These badges are **status symbols for knowledge workers**. They need to feel like something you'd proudly display on a LinkedIn profile, pin to a conference poster, or screenshot to your advisor. Think: GitHub contribution graphs, Strava achievements, Duolingo streaks‚Äîbut for serious academics.

**Core tension to nail:** Intellectual gravitas + visual delight. These can't look like kiddie stickers, but they also can't look like boring certificates. They should feel *earned* and *rare*.

---

## Visual Language

### Overall Aesthetic
- **Style:** Modern heraldic meets tech badge. Think military insignia reimagined by Apple's design team.
- **Shape:** Shield/crest base with distinctive top elements per badge type
- **Dimensionality:** Subtle 3D with soft gradients‚Äînot flat, not skeuomorphic. The "neumorphism lite" that feels premium.
- **Finish:** Metallic sheen on key elements. Gold/silver/bronze hierarchy visible at a glance.

### Color Palette

**Domain Badges (by level):**
- Foundation: Bronze/copper tones (#B87333, #CD7F32) with warm cream accents
- Practice: Silver/steel tones (#C0C0C0, #A8A9AD) with cool white accents
- Mastery: Gold tones (#FFD700, #DAA520) with deep navy accents (#1a1a2e)

**Path Badges:**
- Rich jewel tones that feel prestigious
- Theory Builder: Deep sapphire (#0F52BA)
- Evidence Analyst: Emerald (#046307)
- Integrity Guardian: Ruby (#9B111E)
- Full Researcher: Royal purple (#7851A9)

**Capstone Badge:**
- Black and gold. Period. (#000000, #FFD700)
- This is the PhD of badges. It should look like something you'd frame.

## Domain Badges (21 total)

### Structure
Each domain badge has a consistent base shape with:
1. **Central icon** representing the skill
2. **Level indicator** (ring/border treatment)
3. **Domain number** subtly integrated
4. **Texture/pattern** unique to each domain

### Domain 1: Pattern Recognition & Data Sense

**Concept:** The all-seeing eye that finds signal in noise.

**Visual elements:**
- Central icon: Stylized eye with data points/constellation pattern in the iris
- Radiating lines suggesting pattern detection
- Subtle grid/matrix texture in background
- The eye should feel analytical, not mystical‚Äîmore "machine learning" than "illuminati"

**Tagline for designer:** "Sherlock Holmes meets data science"

---

### Domain 2: Theoretical Positioning

**Concept:** The compass that finds your place in intellectual space.

**Visual elements:**
- Central icon: Compass rose with an academic twist‚Äîpoints could be labeled with subtle theory-related symbols
- Concentric circles suggesting positioning in a field
- Star/north star element for "finding direction"
- Crosshairs or targeting reticle subtly integrated

**Tagline for designer:** "GPS for ideas"

---

### Domain 3: Qualitative Mechanism Extraction

**Concept:** The gears that show *how* things work.

**Visual elements:**
- Central icon: Interlocking gears with visible internal mechanisms
- One gear "cut away" to reveal inner workings
- Speech bubble or quote marks subtly integrated (qualitative = words)
- Clockwork precision feeling

**Tagline for designer:** "Swiss watch meets social science"

---

### Domain 4: Theoretical Framing

**Concept:** The frame that makes the picture.

**Visual elements:**
- Central icon: Ornate picture frame‚Äîbut the "picture" is an abstract geometric pattern
- Multiple nested frames suggesting "framing the framing"
- Perspective lines suggesting multiple viewpoints
- The frame itself is more important than what's inside

**Tagline for designer:** "The frame IS the art"

---

### Domain 5: Epistemological Genre

**Concept:** The ancient scroll that knows discovery from testing.

**Visual elements:**
- Central icon: Unfurling scroll with two distinct "chapters" visible
- One side shows exploration/discovery symbols (question marks, paths diverging)
- Other side shows hypothesis/testing symbols (checkmarks, scales)
- Quill or pen integrated
- Parchment texture

**Tagline for designer:** "Two ways of knowing, one badge"

---

### Domain 6: Adversarial Evidence Handling

**Concept:** The shield that seeks out its own weaknesses.

**Visual elements:**
- Central icon: Shield with deliberate "cracks" or stress-test marks
- Crossed swords or arrows pointing AT the shield (not away)
- "Battle-tested" aesthetic‚Äîweathered but stronger for it
- Balance scale element (weighing evidence)

**Tagline for designer:** "The shield that attacks itself"

---

### Domain 7: Claim Verification

**Concept:** The seal of truth.

**Visual elements:**
- Central icon: Official seal/stamp with verification marks
- Checkmark integrated prominently but elegantly
- Chain links suggesting claim-to-evidence connection
- Notary/authentication aesthetic
- Wax seal texture element

**Tagline for designer:** "Certified, verified, bulletproof"

---

## Level Treatments

### Foundation (Bronze)
- Warm copper/bronze metallic
- Single ring border
- Subtle "1" or "F" watermark
- Clean, unadorned‚Äîyou're just getting started
- **Feel:** "First step on the journey"

### Practice (Silver)
- Cool silver/steel metallic
- Double ring border with subtle pattern between
- Visible wear marks suggesting USE (practice makes progress)
- **Feel:** "Getting your hands dirty"

### Mastery (Gold)
- Rich gold with deep contrasts
- Triple ring border with intricate pattern
- Crown or laurel element added to top of badge
- Subtle glow/radiance effect
- **Feel:** "You've arrived"

## Capstone Badge: AI Supervisor ü§ñ

**THE badge. This is what everyone is working toward.**

### Concept
You are now qualified to supervise AI in research. You can catch what it misses. You are the human in the loop that makes AI-assisted research trustworthy.

### Visual Elements

**Shape:** Circular medallion with octagonal inner frame (8 sides = 7 domains + capstone)

**Central icon:**
- Stylized human figure and AI/robot figure in partnership
- NOT human dominating AI, NOT AI dominating human
- Equal, but human is clearly the *supervisor*
- Eye/oversight element prominent

**Color:**
- Primary: Matte black (#0a0a0a)
- Accents: 24-karat gold (#FFD700)
- Subtle circuit board pattern in the black
- This should look like something Tony Stark would wear

**Border:**
- Seven jewels/nodes representing mastered domains
- Connecting lines showing the full journey
- Capstone element at top (like a crown jewel)

**Special treatment:**
- This badge should have visible "depth"‚Äîlike a challenge coin
- Should feel heavy, substantial, EARNED
- The kind of thing you'd display on a bookshelf

**Tagline:** "The human that AI needs."

**Alt tagline:** "You know what AI doesn't know."

## Technical Specifications

### Dimensions
- Domain badges: 400x400px master (scales down to 64x64 minimum)
- Path badges: 500x600px master (taller aspect ratio)
- Capstone badge: 600x600px master

### File Formats Needed
- SVG (vector, for scaling)
- PNG with transparency (for overlays)
- PNG on white background (for documents)
- Animated version with subtle shimmer (for digital display) ‚Äî optional but üî•

### Accessibility
- Each badge must be recognizable in grayscale
- Distinct silhouettes so colorblind users can differentiate
- Alt text descriptions for screen readers

## Usage Contexts

Design with these display contexts in mind:

1. **Skill-Forge CLI** ‚Äî Small inline display (32-64px)
2. **Progress dashboard** ‚Äî Medium grid display (128px)
3. **LinkedIn/social sharing** ‚Äî Large with badge name visible (400px+)
4. **Certificate/verification page** ‚Äî Full resolution with details
5. **Conference poster** ‚Äî Print-ready vector

## What These Badges Must Make People Feel

1. **"I want that."** Before they've earned it.
2. **"I earned that."** Pride, not just completion.
3. **"Let me show you this."** Shareability built in.
4. **"They have THAT one?!"** Jealousy for the rare ones.
5. **"This means something."** Not participation trophies.

---

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

- ‚ùå Clip art aesthetic
- ‚ùå Flat/boring corporate
- ‚ùå Childish/gamification-cheesy
- ‚ùå So abstract nobody knows what it means
- ‚ùå Text-dependent (should read at glance without words)
- ‚ùå Generic "achievement unlocked" energy
- ‚ùå Busy/cluttered‚Äîthese need to read at 64px

---

## Final Note to Designer

These badges represent real expertise that takes months to develop. PhD students will spend 50-100 hours earning the full set. The badges need to honor that investment.

Think of how a surgeon displays their board certification, or how a pilot displays their wings. It's not flashy, but it MEANS something. That's the energy.

Make people want to work for these.
