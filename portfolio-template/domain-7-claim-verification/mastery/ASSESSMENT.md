## Part 1: Comprehensive Audit (35 min)

### The Paper

**Title:** "Learning by Not Doing: How AI Assistance Undermines Skill Acquisition in Professional Services"

**Abstract:**
> We examine how AI tools affect professional skill development using a natural experiment in the legal profession. When a major legal research platform introduced AI-powered case analysis in 2021, some law firms adopted immediately while others delayed adoption due to client concerns. Comparing associates at early-adopting versus late-adopting firms (n=450), we find that early adopters showed faster document production but scored lower on bar exam simulation exercises administered 18 months later. Qualitative interviews (n=40) reveal that associates at AI-forward firms describe "efficient but shallow" learningâ€”they complete work faster but understand it less deeply. We theorize that AI tools short-circuit the "desirable difficulties" essential for expertise development. Legal education and other professional training programs should incorporate AI-free learning zones to preserve deep skill acquisition.

**Key Findings:**
1. Early AI adopters produced documents 35% faster (p<.001)
2. Early adopters scored 12% lower on bar simulation (p<.05)
3. Interview themes: "efficient but shallow," "I don't really understand why"
4. Firm-level analysis shows no difference in client outcomes

## Evaluation Criteria

### Passing (Mastery Badge Earned)

| Criterion | Requirement |
|-----------|-------------|
| Comprehensive audit | Identifies all major claims |
| Accurate calibration | Correctly assesses claim-evidence match |
| Deep analysis | Identifies subtle overclaiming, not just obvious |
| Reconstruction skill | Preserves contribution while fixing calibration |
| Defense quality | Can defend calibrated claims against pushback |
| Meta-understanding | Articulates when bold claims are appropriate |

### Excellence Indicators

- Catches non-obvious inferential leaps
- Reconstruction is genuinely publishable
- Response to reviewer is persuasive
- Demonstrates sophisticated understanding of claim calibration as rhetorical skill
- Can articulate field-specific norms around claiming

### Failure Modes

- Misses major overclaiming
- Reconstruction strips all contribution
- Unable to defend calibrated claims
- Treats all hedging as equal (doesn't distinguish appropriate from excessive)
- Cannot articulate when bold claims are appropriate
